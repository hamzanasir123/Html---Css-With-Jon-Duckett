HTML heading tags are essential elements for structuring web content, providing a clear and hierarchical organization of information on a webpage. The six heading tags, ranging from h1 to h6, denote different levels of importance, with h1 being the highest and most significant heading, typically used for the main title of a page, while h6 is the least significant. These tags help both users and search engines understand the context and structure of the content. Proper use of heading tags enhances the readability and accessibility of a webpage, allowing users to quickly grasp the main topics and subtopics.
